## **How to Keep Your Sleeping Baby Safe: AAP Policy Explained** 

_Source: HealthyChildren.org — American Academy of Pediatrics_ 

Approximately 3,500 babies die annually in the US from sleep-related causes, primarily SIDS and accidental suffocation/strangulation. 

## **Safe Sleep Environment Recommendations** 

## **Back Sleeping Position** 

Babies who sleep on their backs are much less likely to die suddenly compared to stomach or side positions. Parents needn't worry about choking — babies' airway anatomy prevents this. 

## **Firm Sleep Surface** 

Use cribs, bassinets, or play yards meeting Consumer Product Safety Commission standards. Mattresses must fit tightly with fitted sheets only. Avoid inclined sleepers, Boppy pillows, Dock-a-Tots, and non-approved products. 

## **Room Sharing (No Bed Sharing)** 

Keep baby's sleep area in your room for at least 6 months. This approach can decrease the risk of SIDS by as much as 50%. Never bed share, especially if you've consumed alcohol, used marijuana, or taken sedating medications. 

## **Remove Hazards** 

Eliminate pillows, quilts, blankets, bumper pads, and toys. Use sleep clothing layers instead of blankets. 

## **Temperature Control** 

Avoid overheating. Dress baby one layer more than yourself. Don't use hats indoors after hospital discharge. 

## **Additional SIDS Risk Reduction Strategies** 

- Breastfeed (breast milk provides protection) 

- Offer pacifiers at nap/bedtime (after breastfeeding is established) 

- Attend prenatal care and all well-child visits 

- Avoid smoking, nicotine, alcohol, and drugs during/after pregnancy 

- Ensure baby receives vaccinations 

- Provide supervised tummy time daily (15-30 minutes by 7 weeks) 

- Swaddle safely on back only (discontinue around 3-4 months when rolling begins) 

Source: HealthyChildren.org — American Academy of Pediatrics, 2024
