## **Fever in Children: When to Call the Pediatrician** 

_Source: HealthyChildren.org — American Academy of Pediatrics_ 

## **General Information** 

Fevers typically signal the body's immune response to infection or illness. They're generally harmless and can indicate the child's immune system is functioning properly. 

## **Key Care Measures** 

The most important actions when a child has a fever include ensuring adequate hydration and monitoring for symptoms of serious illness. A positive sign appears when children play and interact normally after receiving comfort medication. 

## **When to Contact Your Pediatrician Immediately** 

Contact your doctor right away if your child has a fever and exhibits any of these conditions: 

- **Appearance/Behavior:** Looks very ill, is unusually drowsy, or is very fussy 

- **Environmental exposure:** Recent time in extremely hot environments (overheated vehicles) 

- **Associated symptoms:** Stiff neck, severe headache, severe sore throat, ear pain, breathing difficulty, unexplained rash, or persistent vomiting/diarrhea 

- **Immune compromise:** Conditions like sickle cell disease, cancer, or medications affecting immunity 

- **Cardiac issues:** Heart problems affecting fever tolerance and heart rate 

- **Seizure history:** Previous seizure activity 

- **Age/Temperature:** Infants under 3 months with temperatures of 100.4°F or higher 

- **Extreme fever:** Temperature exceeding 104°F repeatedly at any age 

## **When to Call for Ongoing Concerns** 

Contact your pediatrician if: 

- Child remains symptomatic after fever reduction 

- Condition worsens 

- Fever persists beyond 24 hours (children under 2) 

- Fever lasts more than 72 hours (children 2+ years)
