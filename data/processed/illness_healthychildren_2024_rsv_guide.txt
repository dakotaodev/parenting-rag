## **RSV: When It's More Than Just a Cold** 

_Source: HealthyChildren.org — American Academy of Pediatrics_ 

## **What is RSV?** 

Respiratory syncytial virus (RSV) is one of the many viruses that cause respiratory illness. The infection typically occurs in the late fall through early spring months. RSV is the most common cause of hospitalization in children under age 1 year, with 2-3 of every 100 infected infants requiring hospital stays. 

## **RSV Symptoms in Babies** 

## **Cold/Upper Respiratory Symptoms** 

- Fever (100.4°F or higher) 

- Dry or wet cough 

- Nasal congestion and runny nose 

- Sneezing 

- Fussiness and poor feeding 

- Hoarse cry or voice 

## **Bronchiolitis/Lower Respiratory Symptoms** 

- Rapid breathing 

- Nostril flaring and head bobbing 

- Grunting during breathing 

- Belly breathing and chest retractions 

- Wheezing 

RSV symptoms are typically at their worst on days 3 through 5 of illness, lasting an average of 7-14 days. 

## **When to Call Your Pediatrician** 

- Bronchiolitis symptoms listed above 

- Signs of dehydration (fewer than 1 wet diaper every 8 hours) 

- Breathing pauses or difficulty 

- Pale, gray, or blue-colored skin 

- Significantly decreased activity 

## **Treatment** 

There is no specific treatment for RSV and medications like steroids and antibiotics don't help. Management focuses on comfort measures: nasal saline with gentle suctioning, cool-mist humidifier, frequent fluids and feedings, acetaminophen or ibuprofen for older infants. Avoid aspirin and cough/cold medications. 

## **Prevention Methods** 

## **New Immunizations** 

- Maternal RSV vaccine (Abrysvo) at 32-36 weeks pregnancy reduces hospitalization risk by 57% in first 6 months 

- Infant RSV immunizations are 80-90% effective, providing antibody protection throughout RSV season 

## **Other Prevention Strategies** 

- Maintain current vaccination schedules 

- Limit baby's exposure to crowds and sick individuals 

- Practice handwashing (20+ seconds with soap and water) 

- Regularly disinfect surfaces 

- Breastfeed if possible 

- Avoid tobacco smoke exposure
